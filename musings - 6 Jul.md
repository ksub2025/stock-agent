**Build and Blog as a Daily Ritual**

Tonight's build almost didn't happen. I was staring at my desk searching for a problem worth solving. I had decided to learn Streamlit, and the rabbit hole of tutorials had eaten my evening. It was closing in on midnight when I gave up on browsing and opened Claude instead: what should we build today with Streamlit?

A few back and forths later, I stopped hunting for a problem and picked one I actually live with. Kite from Zerodha sometimes sucks with holdings data. It shows me my holdings but hides everything I care about. What did I pay? How long have I held? Am I near my exit line? So I decided to build my own answer. Not commission it. Build it.

Four hours later, my phone buzzed. A Telegram message from a bot I created, carrying numbers computed by Python I can now read, checked against rules I wrote into a file myself. My portfolio. My thresholds. My system.

Here is what nobody tells you about the passenger seat: it is comfortable precisely because someone else absorbs all the errors. Tonight I absorbed my own. A file that refused to be found. A sneaky .md extension my text editor smuggled onto a config. A scheduler that said "no crontab for karthi" and made my heart drop, before I learned it just meant nothing here yet. Each error felt like a wall until it became a door.

The AI wrote most of the code. I want to be honest about that. But I chose what to build, questioned every design decision, caught what mattered, and fixed what broke. That division of labor has a name in my head now. The AI is the aircraft. I am learning to fly it. Building is no longer typing every line. Building is knowing what you want, directing the work, reading the errors, and owning the outcome.

Tomorrow at 3:35 PM, five minutes after the market closes, my Mac will wake my script, my script will check my positions, and my phone will buzz whether I remember or not. A small system. Entirely mine.

And that is the ritual now. Build something small. Write it down. Sleep. Repeat.

1+1=11. I keep saying it. Tonight I lived it.
